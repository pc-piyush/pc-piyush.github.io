💡

The Critical Insight: The Commitment K is the Model's Passport

Hospital A publishes one commitment K — a cryptographic fingerprint of its model. Hospital B can run that commitment against any of its own patients, any number of times, without Hospital A ever being involved again. For Patient 1, the arithmetic circuit finds the feature vector satisfies the AKI decision boundary → proof is TRUE, and the onset window is disclosed. For Patient 2, the same circuit finds the vector falls outside the boundary → proof is FALSE, and nothing further is revealed — not even how far below the threshold the patient is. The same commitment K works for every patient. The verdict is patient-specific. The model remains permanently private. This is fundamentally different from sending a model API call — there is no server, no query log, no leakage.
